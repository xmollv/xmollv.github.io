Hi there! 👋 I'm an iOS Developer working at Future Workshops. I was born and raised in Menorca, but I decided to move to Barcelona in 2015. That same year I started writing apps for the iPhone and the iPad, and I've been building iOS apps full-time since then. Still loving it.

How do I work

My bread & butter is Swift, even though I've been writing Objective-C on frameworks and legacy apps. Now that Swift is ABI stable, I'm starting to port a lot of Objective-C code to Swift.

I try to keep the code as simple as possible, and I try to keep the usage of third party dependencies to a minimum. I'm not fond of using VIPER, MVVM, MVP or the new hot architectural pattern that's trendy this week without a compelling reason.

At the time of this writing, I tend to work using MVC + Coordinators as my architectural pattern. For building the UI I don't have a strong opinion, I tend to use what feels right for that view, either code or XIBs. My model layer is always CoreData because I'm a huge fan of NSFetchedResultsController.

I'm also a firm believer in following the Human Interface Guidelines. They're there for a reason.

Personal projects

Abyss

Abyss is the easiest way to store links for later reading. Every link that you store will be available to you on your iPhone, iPad and Mac, forever. We don't require you to log in on any service because we're storing all your data in iCloud. This means that only you have access to it.

  • Sync between devices (iPhone, iPad, Mac) using your personal iCloud.
  • Privacy first. Your data belongs to you, it will never be sent to any external server.
  • Instant search on your entire library.
  • Store any link from any app by using the Share Sheet.
  • Take a peek at any link by simply long-pressing onto it.
  • Swipe actions to quickly mark links as favorite or archive them.
  • Contextual menus to perform advanced operations on your links.
  • Pocket import: Bring all your stored links from Pocket to Abyss.
  • Dynamic Text Size for those that prefer non-standard font sizes.
  • Choose if you want to open your links using Safari or an in-app browser.
  • Dark Mode. You can choose to override the system theme with your preferred one.

Llitgi

Llitgi is an iOS client for Pocket, available on iPhone and iPad as a universal app. It makes browsing & searching for your articles much more pleasant than the original Pocket app. These are some of the key features:

  • Swipe for quick actions. Favorite, Tags, Archive and Delete
  • Save a link from any app using the Share Extension
  • Save a link directly on the app from the pasteboard
  • Add and remove favorite links
  • Archive/Unarchive links
  • Tag links, rename & remove tags in batch
  • Instant search on your entire library
  • No user tracking/analytics on the app
  • Free and Open Source
